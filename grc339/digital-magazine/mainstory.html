Starting out with Polaroid

Are you thinking about beginning your journey with instant photography? This is your one-stop shop to learn all about how to begin, what camera models suit you best, where to buy film, and tips for starting out.

Polaroid photography is a fun and hands-on way to capture moments, with each photo developing right in front of you. This guide will walk you through what kind of camera to start with, how much you can expect to spend on gear and film, where to buy supplies, and simple tips for taking better pictures.

When choosing a camera, beginners should look at Polaroid’s i-Type and 600 series models. The newer i-Type cameras, like the Polaroid Now, are easy to use and cost around $100 to $150. They have built-in rechargeable batteries and features like autofocus and flash. The 600 series cameras are older, but many have been refurbished and are simple to use with their built-in flash and point-and-shoot design. You can often find one online for around $10 to $50 depending on the seller and condition of the camera.

Film is where the cost can add up. A pack of Polaroid film contains 8 photos and costs about $18. You can sometimes save by buying in bundles or looking for sales. Both i-Type and 600 film are easy to find online, at stores like Polaroid’s website, Amazon, or camera shops. Note what film your camera supports, i-type cameras support both 600 and i-type film but 600 camera models do not support i-Type film. Store your film in a cool place and let it warm up to room temperature before use, especially if it's been in the fridge.

Lighting plays a big role in how your photos turn out—instant film works best in bright, natural light. If you’re indoors, using the flash is usually a good idea. Framing can take practice since the viewfinder might not show exactly what the camera captures, especially up close. Some cameras have a simple light or dark setting to help adjust exposure if your photos are coming out too bright or too dark. Every model handles light a bit differently, so don’t be afraid to experiment and learn as you go.

Shooting Polaroid is all about the experience. It’s not just about getting a perfect picture—it's about the fun of trying, learning, and watching the image appear in your hand. Mistakes will happen, and that’s okay. That’s part of what makes it so rewarding.

How did the Polaroid Land cameras work?

Polaroid Land cameras made instant photography possible with self-developing film that produced images in under a minute. A chemical pack spread developer across the photo as it ejected, revealing the image in real time. Just point, shoot, and wait for the magic to appear.

How to scan your exposures

Scanning instant film is the best way to preserve your favorite exposures before they fade. A flatbed scanner captures the image in high detail without damaging the original. It’s a simple step that turns a fragile photo into a lasting digital file.
